Synthetic demonstration data — directionally anchored to public defence trends (Japan 2%-of-GDP buildup, AUKUS, Taiwan special budgets, ROK Defense Reform, NZ DCP-2025) but not real procurement intelligence.

Demand score (0–100) blends near-term budget trajectory, program fit, and acquisition tempo per capability; ALL shows the cross-capability average.

Risk index (0–100) normalizes five dimensions — political/regulatory, competitive saturation, operational/logistics, payment/offset, geo-tension. Toggle RISK to shade by it.

Demand corridors are curated coalition / first-island-chain interoperability links; pulse speed scales with endpoint demand. Illustrative, not trade-flow data.

      How to read this map

      What this is

      A live map scoring where the demand is for a given defense capability across 14 Indo-Pacific markets, and how hard each market is to enter.

      What you're looking at

      • Map shading — the brighter/amber a country, the higher its demand for the capability currently selected. Darker = lower demand.
      • Capability toggle (top) — switch between Counter-UAS, Maritime Autonomy, ISR/Space, C2 & Secure Comms, and Strike-adjacent. The whole map re-shades instantly.
      • Country panel (click any country) — demand score, multi-year budget trajectory, key programs, how you’d actually sell in (FMS / NPOR / Direct Commercial), and a five-dimension risk read.

      Try this

      Click Japan, then switch the capability toggle to Maritime Autonomy and watch the map and the panel change.
